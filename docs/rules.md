# Ekadashi Rules — Satsangi Jeevan, Prakaran 3, Adhyays 31–36

This is the doctrinal basis for the calculator. Shatanand Muni's **Satsangi
Jeevan** gives the most detailed treatment of Ekadashi observance in the
Swaminarayan canon; these adhyays lay out *when* to fast, *when the fast shifts*,
and *when and how to break it*.

---

## 1. The core principle — tithi at sunrise

A tithi is one of 30 lunar "days," each spanning 12° of the Moon's elongation
from the Sun. Ekadashi is the **11th tithi** of each fortnight (paksha).

The day you fast is the day on which **Ekadashi (tithi 11) is present at
sunrise** at *your* location. This is why the correct Gregorian date differs by
place: sunrise in California is ~12.5 hours after sunrise in Gujarat, so the same
Ekadashi tithi can be "caught" by a different calendar date.

---

## 2. Dashami Vedh — the contamination rule (A33)

**Rule:** If the previous tithi, **Dashami (10), is still running at *arunodaya***
— defined as sunrise minus 96 minutes (4 ghatikas) — then the Ekadashi is
**viddha** (pierced / contaminated) and must **not** be fasted on that day. The
fast shifts forward to **Dwadashi (12)**.

Even 1 *pal* (24 seconds) of Dashami bleeding into arunodaya is enough to
contaminate the Ekadashi. Vaishnavas reject all viddha Ekadashis.

```
arunodaya = sunrise − 96 minutes
if Dashami ends after arunodaya  ->  VIDDHA  ->  fast on Dwadashi
else                             ->  SHUDDHA ->  fast today
```

---

## 3. Kshaya Ekadashi — the vanishing tithi (A33)

Because a tithi can be shorter than a solar day, the Ekadashi tithi occasionally
begins *after* one sunrise and ends *before* the next — so it is **absent at
every sunrise**. This is a **kshaya** (lost) Ekadashi.

When this happens the fast is observed on the **Dwadashi day** — i.e., the day
whose sunrise shows tithi 12, immediately following the day that showed Dashami.
On average ~1.2 kshaya Ekadashis occur per year.

```
sunrise day N   = Dasham (10)
sunrise day N+1 = Baras  (12)     <- Ekadashi was skipped; fast on day N+1
```

---

## 4. Vriddhi — the doubled tithi

The opposite of kshaya: a long Ekadashi tithi spans **two** consecutive
sunrises. The first sunrise is often viddha (Dashami vedh) and is rejected; the
fast is observed on the second, shuddha sunrise. Either way the calculator
resolves both readings onto the single correct fast date.

---

## 5. Paran — breaking the fast (A33)

The fast is broken on **Dwadashi**, the day after the observance:

- **After sunrise**, and
- **before the end of the first *pahar*** (the first quarter of daytime).

If Dwadashi is short and ends before the first pahar, break the fast **before
Dwadashi ends**. One must not carry the fast into Trayodashi (13) without cause.

---

## 6. The 18-fold classification (A33)

Each Ekadashi is classified along three axes, giving 18 types:

| Axis | Values |
|------|--------|
| Vedh status | Shuddha / Viddha |
| Ekadashi duration vs 60 ghatikas | Nyuna (short) / Sama (equal) / Adhika (long) |
| Dwadashi duration vs 60 ghatikas | Nyuna / Sama / Adhika |

`2 × 3 × 3 = 18`. Vaishnavas observe only the **9 Shuddha** types on the
Ekadashi day itself; the 9 Viddha types are fasted on Dwadashi. (1 ghatika = 24
minutes; 60 ghatikas = 24 hours.)

---

## 7. The mandatory hierarchy (A32, verses 82–90)

Not everyone can keep all 24. The graded obligation:

| Level | Observance |
|-------|-----------|
| Best | All 24 Ekadashis, *niraahar* (complete food-fast) |
| Good | All 24 with *falahar* (fruits / roots only) |
| Acceptable | The **Chaturmas** Ekadashis (Ashadh Sud → Kartik Sud) |
| Minimum | The 3 mandatory: **Devshayani, Parivartini, Prabodhini** |
| Absolute minimum | **Prabodhini alone** — said to equal the fruit of all 24 |

---

## 8. Chaturmas Ekadashis

The **nine** Ekadashis that fall within the holy monsoon period, from
**Devshayani** (Ashadh Sud 11, when Vishnu enters yoga-nidra) to **Prabodhini**
(Kartik Sud 11, when he awakens) inclusive:

Devshayani → Kamika → Putrada (Shravan) → Aja → Parivartini → Indira →
Pashankusha → **Rama** → Prabodhini.

Note: **Utpanna** (Kartik *Vad* 11) falls *after* Prabodhini and so is **outside**
Chaturmas — it is a regular Ekadashi, not a Chaturmas one.

---

## 9. Adhik-maas Ekadashis

In an intercalary (adhik) lunar month there are two extra Ekadashis (traditionally
*Padmini* in the Sud and *Parama* in the Vad paksha), presided over by
Purushottam. The calculator labels these **Adhik Masa Ekadashi**. Named
Ekadashis like Nirjala are observed in the **nija** (regular) month, not the
adhik one.
