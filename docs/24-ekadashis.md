# The 24 Named Ekadashis — Satsangi Jeevan P3/A35

Each of the 24 Ekadashis of the year has a name, a **presiding deity** (a form of
Bhagwan), and a **shakti** (consort). The 12 Sud (Shukla) Ekadashis belong to the
**Keshavadi** series; the 12 Vad (Krishna) Ekadashis belong to the
**Sankarshanadi** series.

Months are listed in Kartikadi (VS year) order.

## The three mandatory Ekadashis

If one cannot keep all 24, these three are the minimum (SJ P3/A32):

1. **Devshayani** (Ashadh Sud 11) — Vishnu enters yoga-nidra; Chaturmas begins.
2. **Parivartini** (Bhadarvo Sud 11) — Vishnu turns on his side, mid-Chaturmas.
3. **Prabodhini / Dev-uthi** (Kartik Sud 11) — Vishnu awakens; Chaturmas ends.
   Observing **Prabodhini alone** is said to yield the fruit of all 24.

## Adhik-maas

In an intercalary month, the two extra Ekadashis are presided over by
**Purushottam** and are traditionally called **Padmini** (Sud) and **Parama**
(Vad). Named Ekadashis (e.g. Nirjala) are kept in the **nija** regular month.
